Welcome to Calabar

Visitor Information

Calabar is the capital city of Cross River state which is located in the Southern part of Nigeria known as the Niger Delta region.

The city has several museums, a botanical garden, a free trade zone/port, an international airport and seaport, an integrated sports stadium complex, a cultural center, one of the most prominent universities in the country - the University of Calabar, a slave history park and several historical and cultural landmarks.

Demographics

Population: 657,000

Ethnic Groups: Calabar is home to several ethnic groups, including Efik, Efut, and Ibibio

Languages Spoken: English, Efik, Bekwara, and Ejagham

Economy

Calabar's economy is diverse and robust, with key sectors including tourism, agriculture, and manufacturing.

The city is also home to the Calabar Free Trade Zone, attracting businesses from around the globe.

Attractions

From the historical Slave Museum to the beautiful Marina Resort, there's something for everyone in Calabar.

Nature lovers can explore the National Park, while those interested in culture can visit the Cultural Center Complex.

Events

Calabar is known for its lively events calender. The highlight of the year is the Calabar Carnival attracting visitors from around the world.

Other events include the Boat Regatta and the Ekpe Festival
